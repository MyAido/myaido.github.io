Data Subject Rights

Your rights under GDPR, CCPA, and other privacy regulations.

Since Aido stores all data locally on your device and does not collect data on our servers, you have complete control over your information without needing to contact us.

Right to Access

What it means: You have the right to know what data is collected about you.

How to Exercise This Right

  • View Clipboard History: Open Aido → Settings → Clipboard History
  • View Saved Prompts: Open Aido → Saved Prompts tab
  • View API Keys: Settings → API Configuration (masked for security)
  • View App Blacklist: Settings → Disabled Apps

Server Data: We do not store your data on servers, so there is no remote data to access.

Right to Deletion

What it means: You can request deletion of your personal data.

How to Delete Your Data

Data Type How to Delete
Clipboard History Settings → Clear Clipboard History
Saved Prompts Long-press individual prompts → Delete, or Settings → Clear All
API Keys Settings → API Configuration → Remove Keys
All App Data Android Settings → Apps → Aido → Clear Data, or uninstall the app

⚠️ Important: Deleting data is permanent and cannot be reversed. We do not have server backups of your data.

Right to Data Portability

What it means: You can export your data in a common format.

Current Export Options

  • Saved Prompts: Can be manually copied/pasted from the app
  • Settings: Currently no automated export (future feature)

Note: We are working on adding a "Export All Data" feature in a future update.

Right to Rectification

What it means: You can correct inaccurate data.

How to Correct Data

  • Edit saved prompts directly in the app
  • Update API keys in Settings
  • Modify app blacklist as needed

Right to Object

What it means: You can object to certain data processing activities.

How to Object

  • Object to Accessibility Service: Disable in Android Settings → Accessibility
  • Object to Keyboard: Switch to a different keyboard in Android Settings
  • Object to Specific Apps: Add apps to the blacklist in Aido Settings
  • Object to All Processing: Uninstall the app

Right to Restrict Processing

What it means: You can limit how your data is used.

How to Restrict

  • Disable the Accessibility Service (limits text reading)
  • Disable specific features in app settings
  • Use app blacklist to restrict processing in certain apps
  • Turn off clipboard history to prevent local storage

International Users

GDPR (European Union)

Aido is GDPR-compliant. All rights above apply to EU users. Since we don't transfer data to third parties (except AI providers you choose), there are no cross-border data transfer concerns with Aido's own operations.

CCPA (California)

California residents have the right to:

  • Know what personal information is collected (see Right to Access)
  • Delete personal information (see Right to Deletion)
  • Opt-out of sale of personal information (We do NOT sell data)
  • Non-discrimination for exercising privacy rights

Other Regions

We respect privacy laws in all jurisdictions. Contact us if you have specific regional privacy questions.

How to Contact Us

For data subject requests or privacy questions:

Email: aiqknow@gmail.com

We will respond within:

  • GDPR requests: 30 days (can be extended to 60 days for complex requests)
  • CCPA requests: 45 days (can be extended to 90 days)
  • General inquiries: 48 hours

Your Data, Your Control

Aido's local-first architecture means you have immediate control over your data without waiting for us to process requests. Simply use the app's built-in settings or Android's system settings to manage your information.
